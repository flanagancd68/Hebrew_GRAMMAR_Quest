    • 2.1 Vowels that are not vowel letters
      • Vowels come in three types: Long, Short, Reduced | Vowels come in five classes: A, E, I, O, U
    • 2.2 Vocal and Silent Sheva
      • Both are written as בְּ | VOCAL Sheva is a REDUCED vowel | SILENT Sheva is NOT A Vowel
    • 2.3 Vowel letters
      • Vowel letters use a consonant plus a nikkud to form a vowel
    • 2.4 “Defective” and “plene” spelling
      • In “defective” spelling, letter vowels can sometimes drop their letter and take on the corresponding non-letter vowel. The meaning of the word doesn’t change.
    • 2.5 The Daghesh Forte
      • A Daghesh Forte doubles the consonant
    • 2.6 Daghesh Forte Rule
      • “A Daghesh is a Forte if, and only if, it’s preceded by a vowel that is not a Sheva.” —John Beckman

    • Seven Practical Points for Lesson 3
      • Lesson Prayer
    • Equipment Check
      • The authors of Basics of Biblical Hebrew believe Lesson 3 could be the most challenging chapter in the book.
    • 3.1 Hebrew Syllables
      • Every syllable begins with one consonant and has only one vowel
      • There are only open or closed syllables
    • 3.2 Hebrew Word Accents
      • Most frequently, Hebrew words are stressed on the last syllable.
      • If not, then the accent will be on the next-to-last syllable
      • Tonic, Pretonic, and Propretonic Syllables
    • 3.3 Rules for Silent Sheva
      • SILENT when the previous vowel is short
      • SILENT when the first of two consecutive Shevas within a word
      • SILENT when at the end of a word

Get Hebrew Healthy

  • In Israel, there are a lot of hills and tourists must be in shape to do a lot of walking. Hebrew also has a lot of grammatical hills to climb, so we need to get Hebrew Healthy.
  • To get in Hebrew shape, we won’t go to a physical gym, but there is a powerful piece of equipment that will build your Hebrew strength and endurance. It’s called Anki.
    • The majority of your work in this course will be doing what we call Anki Aerobics.
    • There will be three phases of Anki Aerobics in each lesson: Vocabulary, Grammar concepts, reinforcing and expanding on the Seven Practical Points, and Study Verses (Bible verse translation).
    • Beginning with Lesson 3, we will also have a brief Word Warm-ups video designed to give you an audio and visual introduction to that lesson’s vocabulary list.
    • There is an entire section in the Appendices devoted to Anki as well as the Anki official documentation if you’d like to read more.
