  • 2 Hebrew Vowels
    • Seven Practical Points for Lesson 2
    • Equipment Check
    • 2.1 Vowels that are not vowel letters
      • Vowels come in three types: Long, Short, Reduced | Vowels come in five classes: A, E, I, O, U
    • 2.2 Vocal silent sheva
      • 2.2.1 Both are written as: בְּ | VOCAL Sheva is a REDUCED vowel | SILENT Sheva is NOT A Vowel
    • 2.3 Vowel letters
      • Vowel letters use a consonant plus a nikkud to form a vowel
    • 2.4 “Defective” and “plene” writing
      • In “defective” writing, letter vowels can sometimes drop their letter and take on the corresponding non-letter vowel. The meaning of word doesn’t change.
    • 2.5 The Daghesh Forte
      • A Daghesh Forte doubles the consonant
    • 2.6 Daghesh Forte Rule
      • “A Daghesh is a Forte if, and only if, it’s preceeded by a vowel that is not a sheva.” —John Beckman
    • 2.7 Gutturals and Resh reject Daghesh Forte

  • 3 Syllabification and Pronunciation
    • Seven Practical Points for Lesson 2
    • Equipment Check
      • The authors of Basics of Biblical Hebrew believe Lesson 3 could be the most difficult and time-consuming chapter in the book.
    • 3.1 Hebrew Syllables
      • Every syllable begins with one consonant and has only one vowel
      • There are only open or closed syllables
    • 3.2 Hebrew Word Accents
      • Most frequently, Hebrew words are stressed on the last syllable.
      • If not, then the the accent will be on the next-to-last syllable
      • Tonic, Pretonic, and Propretonic Syllables
    • 3.3 Silent Sheva
      • SILENT when previous vowel is short
      • SILENT when the first of two consecutive Shevas within a word
      • SILENT when at the end of a word
    • 3.4 Vocal Sheva
      • VOCAL when the initial Sheva in a word
      • VOCAL when the second of two consecutive Shevas within a word
      • VOCAL when under a Daghesh Forte
      • VOCAL after an unaccented long vowel
    • 3.5 Hebrew Diphthong = Accented Pathach-Yod-Hiriq
    • 3.6 Vowels and Syllable Preference
    • 3.7 Qamets Hatuf, Furtive Pathach, Quiescent Alef
      • Qamets Hatuf ONLY occurs in a Closed AND Unaccented syllable
      • 3.7.1 Furtive Pathach under final ח or ע is said BEFORE the guttural letter and is not a full vowel
      • Quiescent Aleph is silent, neither a consonant nor a vowel
